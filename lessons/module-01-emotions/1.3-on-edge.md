# Lesson 1.3 — On Edge

**Theme:** Emotions & Mood  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *on edge*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
You have a big job interview in two hours. You can't relax. Every small noise irritates you. You keep checking your phone.

What idiom could describe this nervous, tense feeling? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "I'm on edge." ⚡

Meaning: nervous, tense, and easily irritated — like you're waiting for something to go wrong.

Pattern:
• "I get on edge before big presentations."
• "Everyone was on edge during the exam."
• "The silence in the room put me on edge."

Note: "on edge" is about nervous tension, not just sadness. It often includes irritability.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) When do YOU usually feel on edge?

Write one sentence — or record a short voice note. 🎙️

Example: "I'm always on edge the night before a big exam."
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- "On edge" can describe a person OR an atmosphere: "The whole office was on edge."
- Contrast with "on pins and needles" (lesson extension idea) — both express nervous waiting.
