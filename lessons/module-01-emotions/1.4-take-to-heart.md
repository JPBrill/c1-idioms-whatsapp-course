# Lesson 1.4 — Take Something to Heart

**Theme:** Emotions & Mood  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *take something to heart*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
Your manager gives you some feedback. It's not personal — they're just trying to help. But you keep thinking about it all day and feel genuinely hurt.

What idiom can describe this kind of deep emotional reaction? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "take something to heart" 💔

Meaning: be deeply affected by something — especially criticism or a comment.

Pattern:
• "Try not to take it to heart."
• "She really took his words to heart."
• "He took the feedback to heart and improved."

Note: It can be negative (feeling hurt) OR positive (taking advice seriously and acting on it).
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence where someone takes something to heart.

Example: "He took the teacher's comments to heart and rewrote the whole essay."

2) Have you ever taken something to heart that you probably shouldn't have? Tell us in one or two sentences. 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Great idiom for IELTS Speaking Part 2 and 3 (personal experience questions).
- Encourage students to notice the double meaning: deeply hurt vs. taking advice seriously.
