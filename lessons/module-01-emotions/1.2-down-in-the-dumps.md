# Lesson 1.2 — Down in the Dumps

**Theme:** Emotions & Mood  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *down in the dumps*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
You failed your driving test again. You feel sad, disappointed — and a little bit hopeless.

What idiom could you use for this low, negative mood? 🤔

(Reply with your guess first!)
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Native speakers say: "I'm feeling down in the dumps." 😔

Meaning: feeling very sad or depressed — usually for a short period of time.

Pattern:
• "He's been down in the dumps since ..."
• "I get down in the dumps when ..."
• "She seemed really down in the dumps today."

Note: This describes a temporary low mood, not a serious medical condition.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write one sentence using "down in the dumps" about a real or imaginary situation.

Example: "I was down in the dumps for a week after I missed the deadline."

2) Bonus: What usually cheers you up when you feel down in the dumps? Answer in one sentence. 🎙️
```
