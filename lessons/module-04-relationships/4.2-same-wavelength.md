# Lesson 4.2 — On the Same Wavelength

**Theme:** Relationships & Conflict  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *on the same wavelength*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
You and your co-teacher plan every lesson together. You never argue. When one of you starts a sentence, the other finishes it. You always know what the other is thinking.

What idiom could describe this kind of deep mutual understanding? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "on the same wavelength" 📡

Meaning: thinking in a similar way — understanding each other naturally without much explanation.

Pattern:
• "My co-teacher and I are on the same wavelength."
• "We need to get on the same wavelength before the meeting."
• "They're not on the same wavelength — communication is always difficult."

Note: Can refer to ideas, values, or working style — not just personality.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence using "on the same wavelength".

Example: "My business partner and I are completely on the same wavelength about the brand direction."

2) Bonus: Who in your life are you most on the same wavelength with? Why? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Useful contrast: "We're not on the same wavelength" — great for describing workplace conflict.
- Works well in IELTS Speaking Part 3 (teamwork, relationships, leadership).
