# Lesson 4.3 — Give Someone the Cold Shoulder

**Theme:** Relationships & Conflict  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *give someone the cold shoulder*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
You and a friend had a big argument. Now, when you walk into the room, they look away, don't reply to your messages, and pretend you don't exist. They're doing it deliberately.

What idiom describes what your friend is doing? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "give someone the cold shoulder" 🥶

Meaning: deliberately ignore someone or be unfriendly as a way of showing displeasure.

Pattern:
• "After the argument, he gave me the cold shoulder."
• "She's been giving him the cold shoulder all week."
• "Why is everyone giving me the cold shoulder?"

Note: The "cold shoulder" is the treatment you receive — it's always deliberate, not accidental.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence where someone gives someone the cold shoulder.

Example: "After the disagreement, the whole team gave him the cold shoulder for days."

2) Bonus: Have you ever given someone the cold shoulder — or received it? How did it feel? 🎙️
```
