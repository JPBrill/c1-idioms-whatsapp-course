# Lesson 4.4 — In Hot Water

**Theme:** Relationships & Conflict  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *in hot water*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
An employee forgets to submit a report, misses a deadline, and then argues with a client. The manager calls them into the office. Things are serious.

What idiom could describe the employee's situation? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "in hot water" 🌡️

Meaning: in serious trouble — usually because of something you did wrong.

Pattern:
• "I'll be in hot water if I miss this deadline."
• "He's in hot water with the boss again."
• "The company found itself in hot water after the scandal."

Note: You can be in hot water with a person ("with my parents") or because of a situation.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence where someone is in hot water.

Example: "She was in hot water with her supervisor after arriving late three times in a row."

2) Bonus: Describe a situation (real or imaginary) where someone gets themselves into hot water. 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Can be used for individuals, companies, and even governments.
- Pairs well with "in trouble" as a simpler synonym — great for showing the upgrade to C1 language.
