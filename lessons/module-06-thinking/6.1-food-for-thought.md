# Lesson 6.1 — Food for Thought

**Theme:** Thinking & Learning  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *food for thought*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
A speaker gives a talk about the future of work. After the talk, you can't stop thinking about the ideas. They've given you a lot to reflect on, question, and explore further.

What idiom could describe what the speaker gave you? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "food for thought" 🧠

Meaning: something that makes you think carefully — ideas or information worth reflecting on.

Pattern:
• "Her comment gave me a lot of food for thought."
• "That documentary was real food for thought."
• "The report raises some interesting points — food for thought."

Note: Uncountable noun — you can't say "a food for thought." Say "some food for thought" or "real food for thought."
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence using "food for thought".

Example: "His question about my career goals gave me plenty of food for thought."

2) Bonus: What's the last thing that gave you genuine food for thought? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Natural in academic writing and formal speech — excellent for IELTS.
- Common closing phrase: "This is certainly food for thought." — great for essay conclusions.
