# Lesson 6.3 — Hit the Nail on the Head

**Theme:** Thinking & Learning  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *hit the nail on the head*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
The team has been arguing about why sales are dropping. Then one person says: "The problem isn't the product — it's the pricing. We're 30% more expensive than competitors with a similar offer." Suddenly everyone agrees. That was exactly it.

What idiom describes what that person did? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "hit the nail on the head" 🔨

Meaning: describe or identify something exactly right — the perfect explanation or answer.

Pattern:
• "You've hit the nail on the head."
• "She hit the nail on the head with that analysis."
• "That comment really hit the nail on the head."

Note: Used as a compliment — to tell someone they've identified something perfectly.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence where someone hits the nail on the head.

Example: "When he said the real issue was communication, he hit the nail on the head."

2) Bonus: When was the last time someone hit the nail on the head about YOU or your situation? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Great for discussion and debate contexts — a natural response to a strong point.
- Can be used to agree in academic writing: "As [author] correctly identifies..." is the formal equivalent.
