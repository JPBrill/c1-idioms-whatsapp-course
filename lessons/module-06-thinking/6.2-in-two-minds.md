# Lesson 6.2 — In Two Minds

**Theme:** Thinking & Learning  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *in two minds*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
You've been offered a job in another city. It's a great opportunity — better pay, more responsibility. But it means leaving your family and friends behind. You genuinely can't decide what to do.

What idiom describes this feeling of being stuck between two options? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "in two minds" 🔀

Meaning: unable to decide between two options — having conflicting feelings or thoughts.

Pattern:
• "I'm in two minds about changing jobs."
• "She was in two minds about whether to go."
• "Even now, I'm still in two minds."

Note: British English — American English equivalent is "of two minds".
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence about something you're (or were) in two minds about.

Example: "I was in two minds about studying abroad — exciting, but scary."

2) Bonus: Tell us about a tough decision you've been in two minds about. How did you decide? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Excellent for IELTS Speaking Part 2 (personal experience/difficult decisions).
- Pairs naturally with "weigh up the pros and cons" as a complementary C1 expression.
