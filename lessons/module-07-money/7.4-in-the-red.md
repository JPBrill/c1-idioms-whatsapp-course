# Lesson 7.4 — In the Red

**Theme:** Money & Value  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *in the red*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
The company had a difficult year. Sales dropped. Costs rose. By December, they were spending more than they earned, and their bank balance showed a negative figure.

What idiom describes this financial situation? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "in the red" 🔴

Meaning: owing money; spending more than you have; having a negative bank balance.

Pattern:
• "The company has been in the red for months."
• "We can't afford that — we're already in the red."
• "They finally moved out of the red after the new contract."

Note: Opposite = "in the black" (profitable / positive balance). Both are worth teaching together.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence using "in the red".

Example: "The start-up spent heavily in its first year and stayed in the red until its third."

2) Bonus: Why do you think some businesses stay in the red for years before becoming profitable? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Pair with "in the black" as a natural contrast (profitable).
- Origin: old accounting practice of writing debts in red ink.
