# Lesson 7.1 — Cost an Arm and a Leg

**Theme:** Money & Value  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *cost an arm and a leg*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
You need a new laptop. The one you want is exactly what you need — but the price is shocking. You can barely believe they're charging that much for it. It's extremely expensive.

What idiom could describe this price? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "cost an arm and a leg" 💸

Meaning: be very expensive — much more than expected or reasonable.

Pattern:
• "That car costs an arm and a leg."
• "Private schools cost an arm and a leg."
• "It doesn't have to cost an arm and a leg to look professional."

Note: Always used hyperbolically — not literally. You still have your limbs.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence using "cost an arm and a leg".

Example: "Eating out in central London costs an arm and a leg."

2) Bonus: Name something that costs an arm and a leg in your country. Is it worth it? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Very high frequency and instantly recognised.
- Fun contrast: "won't break the bank" = affordable (a great C1 companion expression).
