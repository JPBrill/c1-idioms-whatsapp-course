## 📱 Activity 2 — Odd One Out

```
🧠 ACTIVITY 2: Odd One Out

In each group, which sentence uses the idiom INCORRECTLY?

Group A:
a) "That holiday cost an arm and a leg — it was about €50."
b) "Healthcare costs an arm and a leg in some countries."
c) "Getting a lawyer costs an arm and a leg."

Group B:
a) "In the red means you owe more than you have."
b) "She's in the red — she has €5,000 in savings."
c) "The business has been in the red since the pandemic."

Answers below ⬇️
```

---

## 📱 Activity 2 — Answers

```
✅ ANSWERS:
Group A: (a) — €50 is not expensive enough to "cost an arm and a leg"
Group B: (b) — having €5,000 in savings means you're in the black, not the red

Score? 🎯
```
