# Lesson 7.3 — Tighten Your Belt

**Theme:** Money & Value  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *tighten your belt*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
After losing his job, Marcus had to make big changes. No eating out. No new clothes. No weekend trips. He tracked every cent and cut everything non-essential.

What idiom describes what Marcus had to do? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "tighten your belt" 🔧

Meaning: spend less money because you have less available — live more frugally.

Pattern:
• "We had to tighten our belts after the company closed."
• "The whole country was tightening its belt during the recession."
• "It's time to tighten your belt and cut back on luxuries."

Note: Can apply to individuals, families, businesses, or entire economies.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence about tightening your belt.

Example: "After the unexpected repair bill, we had to tighten our belts for a few months."

2) Bonus: What's the first thing you cut when you need to tighten your belt? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Works for micro and macro contexts — personal finance and economics essays.
- Strong IELTS Task 2 idiom for economy / austerity topics.
