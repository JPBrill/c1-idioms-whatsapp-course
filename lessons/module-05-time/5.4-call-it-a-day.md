# Lesson 5.4 — Call It a Day

**Theme:** Time & Planning  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *call it a day*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
Your team has been working since 8am. It's 6pm. Everyone is tired, the main tasks are done, and pushing on now would produce poor results. It's time to stop for today.

What idiom could the manager say to end the session? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "call it a day" 🌅

Meaning: decide to stop working on something for now — often because you've done enough.

Pattern:
• "Let's call it a day and continue tomorrow."
• "After three hours of revising, I decided to call it a day."
• "We're making good progress — let's call it a day here."

Note: Implies a natural stopping point, not giving up. Also: "call it a night" (for evening work).
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence using "call it a day".

Example: "After finishing the third chapter, I called it a day and made dinner."

2) Bonus: What's your signal that it's time to call it a day when you're studying or working? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Very natural in spoken and written English.
- "Call it a night" is the evening variant — worth mentioning as an extension.
