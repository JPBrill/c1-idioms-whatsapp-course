# Lesson 5.1 — Last-Minute

**Theme:** Time & Planning  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *last-minute*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
He never prepares early. He always starts his assignments the night before they're due. He books flights the day of travel. He buys gifts on the way to the party.

What idiom (used as an adjective) describes this person's style? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "last-minute" ⏱️

Meaning: done or happening just before the final deadline — with almost no time to spare.

Pattern:
• "He's always doing last-minute preparation."
• "I made some last-minute changes to the report."
• "We found a last-minute solution just in time."

Note: Used as an adjective before a noun. Can also say: "at the last minute" (adverbial):
"She cancelled at the last minute."
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence using "last-minute" or "at the last minute".

Example: "I always do last-minute revision the morning of an exam."

2) Bonus: Are you a last-minute person or an early planner? Be honest! 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Covers two structures: adjective ("last-minute changes") and adverbial ("at the last minute").
- Very high frequency — students will encounter this constantly in real English.
