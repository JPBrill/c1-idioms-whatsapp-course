# Lesson 5.3 — Around the Corner

**Theme:** Time & Planning  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *around the corner*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
It's already mid-November. Christmas markets are opening. Shops are filling with decorations. Everyone is talking about holidays and family plans. The festive season is almost here.

What idiom could you use to say something is coming very soon? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "around the corner" 📅

Meaning: happening or arriving very soon — almost here.

Pattern:
• "The deadline is just around the corner."
• "Change is right around the corner."
• "Summer is around the corner — are you ready?"

Note: The word "just" or "right" before it makes it feel even more immediate: "just around the corner."
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence using "around the corner" about something upcoming in your life.

Example: "My IELTS exam is just around the corner, so I've been studying intensively."

2) Bonus: What's around the corner for you in the next few weeks? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Common in motivational contexts: "Success is right around the corner."
- Also literal — it's idiomatically distinct, so worth clarifying the figurative use.
