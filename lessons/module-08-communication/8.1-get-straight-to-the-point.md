# Lesson 8.1 — Get Straight to the Point

**Theme:** Communication & Honesty  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *get straight to the point*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
Your boss has five minutes before the next meeting. You need to explain a problem and get a decision. You skip all the background, all the small talk, and immediately say exactly what you need.

What idiom describes this communication style? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "get straight to the point" 🎯

Meaning: talk about the main issue immediately — without unnecessary introduction.

Pattern:
• "Let me get straight to the point: we need to cut costs."
• "He always gets straight to the point in meetings."
• "Can you get straight to the point? I only have two minutes."

Note: "cut to the chase" is a strong American equivalent — worth teaching together.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence using "get straight to the point".

Example: "She got straight to the point in her presentation and the audience appreciated it."

2) Bonus: Do you prefer people who get straight to the point, or do you like more context first? Why? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Great for professional and academic writing openers.
- Common IELTS Task 2 writing tip: get straight to the point in your introduction.
