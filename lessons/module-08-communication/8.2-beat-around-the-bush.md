# Lesson 8.2 — Beat Around the Bush

**Theme:** Communication & Honesty  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *beat around the bush*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
Your flatmate needs to move out, but instead of saying it directly, he talks about "situations changing" and "needing different energy" and "life transitions" for 20 minutes without ever saying what he actually means.

What idiom describes what he's doing? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "beat around the bush" 🌿

Meaning: avoid talking about the main topic — talk around the issue without saying it directly.

Pattern:
• "Stop beating around the bush and tell me the truth."
• "She spent ten minutes beating around the bush before getting to the point."
• "Don't beat around the bush — just say it."

Note: Often said as a command or criticism: "Stop beating around the bush!"
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence where someone beats around the bush.

Example: "He beat around the bush for so long that the meeting ran 30 minutes over time."

2) Bonus: Why do people beat around the bush? Give one reason in your own words. 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Natural contrast with "get straight to the point" (lesson 8.1) — teach as a pair.
- Common in IELTS feedback: examiners value direct, clear communication.
