# Lesson 8.3 — Keep Someone in the Loop

**Theme:** Communication & Honesty  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *keep someone in the loop*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
A project is moving fast. The manager makes sure every team member gets regular updates — emails, quick messages, brief meetings. Nobody is ever surprised by changes because everyone is always informed.

What idiom describes what the manager is doing? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "keep someone in the loop" 🔄

Meaning: make sure someone receives regular updates and information about something.

Pattern:
• "Please keep me in the loop about the project."
• "I'll keep you in the loop as things develop."
• "Nobody kept us in the loop — we had no idea what was happening."

Note: Opposite = "left out of the loop" (not kept informed).
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence using "keep in the loop".

Example: "I always keep my clients in the loop so there are no surprises at the end."

2) Bonus: Have you ever been left out of the loop at work or at school? How did it affect things? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Very common in professional communication — emails, meetings, project management.
- Extend: "loop someone in" (verb) = CC them in an email / add them to a communication.
