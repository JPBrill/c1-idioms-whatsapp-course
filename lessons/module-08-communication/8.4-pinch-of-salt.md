# Lesson 8.4 — Take Something with a Pinch of Salt

**Theme:** Communication & Honesty  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *take something with a pinch of salt*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
Your colleague tells you he ran a marathon last weekend, speaks six languages, and once had dinner with a famous actor. You smile and nod — but you don't fully believe everything he says.

What idiom describes your reaction to his stories? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "take something with a pinch of salt" 🧂

Meaning: not completely believe something — be sceptical about whether it's entirely true.

Pattern:
• "Take his stories with a pinch of salt."
• "You should take those statistics with a pinch of salt."
• "I take online reviews with a pinch of salt."

Note: American English uses "grain of salt" instead of "pinch of salt" — both are correct.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence using "take with a pinch of salt".

Example: "I take social media influencer reviews with a pinch of salt — they're usually paid."

2) Bonus: What kind of information do you always take with a pinch of salt? Why? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Excellent for IELTS Writing Task 2 essays on media, advertising, and critical thinking.
- The "grain of salt" American variant is worth mentioning for awareness.
