# Lesson 2.4 — Pull Your Weight

**Theme:** Work & Productivity  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *pull your weight*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
You're in a group project. Two people do almost everything. One person keeps missing meetings, misses deadlines, and never contributes. The rest of the group is angry.

What idiom describes what that person is NOT doing? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "pull your weight" ⚖️

Meaning: do your fair share of the work — contribute equally to a group or team.

Pattern:
• "Everyone needs to pull their weight."
• "He isn't pulling his weight on this project."
• "If you don't pull your weight, the team suffers."

Note: Usually used in negative form to criticise someone who isn't contributing enough.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence where someone is NOT pulling their weight.

Example: "It was obvious that one member wasn't pulling their weight, so the manager had to step in."

2) Bonus opinion question: Should group work be graded individually or as a team? Use "pull their weight" in your answer. 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Strong idiom for IELTS Writing Task 2 essays on teamwork or workplace fairness.
- Also useful in IELTS Speaking Part 3: "Do you think all team members always pull their weight?"
