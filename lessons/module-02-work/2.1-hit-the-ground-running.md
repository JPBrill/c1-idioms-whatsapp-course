# Lesson 2.1 — Hit the Ground Running

**Theme:** Work & Productivity  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *hit the ground running*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
It's your first day in a new job. Instead of spending weeks slowly settling in, you start working immediately, get things done fast, and impress everyone around you.

What idiom could describe this kind of strong, energetic start? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "hit the ground running" 🏃

Meaning: start something quickly and with a lot of energy — without wasting time warming up.

Pattern:
• "We need someone who can hit the ground running."
• "She hit the ground running in her new role."
• "I want to hit the ground running this semester."

Note: Often used in job interviews and work contexts. It signals readiness and confidence.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write one sentence with "hit the ground running" about work or study.

Example: "After the holidays, I want to hit the ground running with my thesis."

2) Bonus: When was a time you really DID hit the ground running? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Excellent for IELTS Speaking Part 3 (work/ambition topics).
- Common in job adverts: "We're looking for someone who can hit the ground running."
