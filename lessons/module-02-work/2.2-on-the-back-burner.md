# Lesson 2.2 — On the Back Burner

**Theme:** Work & Productivity  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *on the back burner*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
You have a personal project you're excited about — maybe an online course or a business idea. But work and family are more urgent right now. You decide to delay it for a while.

What idiom could describe this decision to pause something? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "put something on the back burner" 🔥

Meaning: decide to deal with something later because other things are more urgent right now.

Pattern:
• "I've put my Spanish course on the back burner."
• "The plan is on the back burner for now."
• "We'll have to put that idea on the back burner."

Origin: From cooking — the back burner on a stove is for things you're keeping warm but not actively working on.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) What have YOU put on the back burner recently?

Write one sentence.

Example: "I've put my fitness routine on the back burner since the project started."

2) Bonus: Is it a good idea to always put personal goals on the back burner? Tell us your opinion! 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Great for IELTS Writing Task 2 (prioritisation / work-life balance topics).
- Opposite idea: "bring something to the front burner" (less common but understood).
