# Week 2 Review — Work & Productivity

**Idioms covered:**
- hit the ground running
- on the back burner
- up to your ears in work
- pull your weight

---

## 📱 Weekly Review Message

```

You've learned 4 work idioms this week:
✅ hit the ground running
✅ on the back burner

✅ pull your weight

```

---

## 📱 Activity 1 — Workplace Scenario

```
✍️ ACTIVITY 1: Workplace Scenario

You're a new manager writing a message to your team.
Use ALL FOUR idioms from this week naturally in your message.

You can start like this:
"Hi team, I know some of you are..."

Post your message here! 👇
```

---

## 📱 Activity 2 — Match the Idiom

```
🧠 ACTIVITY 2: Match the situation to the idiom

A) hit the ground running
B) on the back burner
C) up to your ears in work
D) pull your weight

1) "I've had to delay my gym routine because of the project."
2) "She joined the company on Monday and had already closed two deals by Friday."
3) "I have 200 emails, three reports, and a presentation — all due today."
4) "Everyone in the team works hard except him."

Answers below ⬇️
```

---

## 📱 Activity 2 — Answers

```
✅ ANSWERS:
1) B — on the back burner
2) A — hit the ground running
3) C — up to your ears in work
4) D — pull your weight

How many did you get right? 🎯
```

## Teacher Notes
- The "Workplace Scenario" task works well as a voice note for speaking practice.
- For IELTS prep: ask students to use these in a Part 2 talk about their job or studies.
