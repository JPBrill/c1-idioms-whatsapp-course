# Lesson 2.3 — Up to Your Ears in Work

**Theme:** Work & Productivity  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *up to your ears in work*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
Your boss gives you three extra tasks. You already have two deadlines this week. Your inbox is full. You feel completely overwhelmed and buried under everything.

What idiom could you use for this overloaded situation? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "I'm up to my ears in work." 😵

Meaning: extremely busy — so much work you feel overwhelmed by it.

Variations (all natural):
• "up to my neck in work"
• "up to my eyes in work"
• "up to my ears in emails / deadlines / problems"

Note: You can swap "work" for anything overwhelming:
"I'm up to my ears in marking." (teachers)
"I'm up to my neck in debt." (financial stress)
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write one sentence about a time you were up to your ears in work (or study).

Example: "During exam season I was up to my ears in revision."

2) Bonus: How do you cope when you're up to your ears in work? Answer in 1–2 sentences. 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- The body-part variations (ears / neck / eyes) all sound natural — worth mentioning.
- Encourage students to use the flexible "up to my [body part] in [noun]" pattern creatively.
