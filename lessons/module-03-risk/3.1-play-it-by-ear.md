# Lesson 3.1 — Play It by Ear

**Theme:** Risk & Decision-Making  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *play it by ear*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
Your friend asks what the plan is for Saturday. You're not sure — it depends on the weather, how tired everyone is, and who's available. You decide not to plan in advance and just see what happens.

What idiom describes this approach? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "play it by ear" 🎵

Meaning: decide what to do as events happen, rather than making a fixed plan in advance.

Pattern:
• "Let's play it by ear and see how the day goes."
• "I'm not sure yet — I'll just play it by ear."
• "We can't plan everything — sometimes you have to play it by ear."

Origin: From music — playing by ear means playing without sheet music, adapting as you go.
```

---
