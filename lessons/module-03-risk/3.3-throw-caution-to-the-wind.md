# Lesson 3.3 — Throw Caution to the Wind

**Theme:** Risk & Decision-Making  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *throw caution to the wind*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
She's always been careful, safe, and sensible. Then one day — completely out of character — she quits her stable job, books a one-way ticket to South America, and starts a business with her savings.

What idiom describes this sudden change in behaviour? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "throw caution to the wind" 💨

Meaning: stop being careful and do something risky or reckless — often spontaneously.

Pattern:
• "She threw caution to the wind and quit her job."
• "Sometimes you just have to throw caution to the wind."
• "He threw caution to the wind and invested everything."

Note: Suggests a strong, often dramatic decision to ignore the risks.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence where someone throws caution to the wind.

Example: "They threw caution to the wind and launched the product before it was fully ready."

2) Bonus: Is it ever a good idea to throw caution to the wind? When? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Great idiom for narrative writing and IELTS Task 2 arguments about risk vs. caution.
- Opposite: "err on the side of caution" — a great C1 contrast phrase to teach together.
