# Lesson 3.4 — Touch-and-Go

**Theme:** Risk & Decision-Making  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *touch-and-go*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
The patient was in surgery for five hours. The doctors couldn't guarantee success. At every point, it could have gone either way. Everyone outside was waiting nervously.

What idiom could describe how uncertain the situation was? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "touch-and-go" ⚠️

Meaning: a situation that is very uncertain — it could easily succeed or fail right up until the end.

Pattern:
• "It was touch-and-go for a while, but she recovered."
• "The exam was touch-and-go, but I passed."
• "The whole negotiation was touch-and-go."

Note: Often used after a crisis to describe how uncertain it felt: "It was touch-and-go."
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence using "touch-and-go" about a tense situation.

Example: "It was touch-and-go whether the flight would depart on time."

2) Bonus: Tell us about a touch-and-go moment in your life — real or imaginary. 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Can be used as an adjective: "a touch-and-go situation" or predicatively: "It was touch-and-go."
- Particularly vivid in medical, sporting, and exam contexts.
