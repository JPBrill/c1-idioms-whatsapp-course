# Lesson 3.2 — Go Out on a Limb

**Theme:** Risk & Decision-Making  
**Level:** C1  
**Idiom:** *go out on a limb*

---

## 📱 Message 1 — Context & Guess

```
Imagine this:
Everyone in the meeting disagrees with the new strategy. Then one person speaks up, defends the idea publicly, and takes full responsibility if it fails. Nobody else was willing to do that.

What idiom describes what that person did? 🤔
```

---

## 📱 Message 2 — Reveal & Explanation

```
Idiom: "go out on a limb" 🌳

Meaning: take a risk by supporting something or someone that might fail — putting yourself in a vulnerable position.

Pattern:
• "I'm going out on a limb and saying we'll win."
• "She went out on a limb to support his idea."
• "I don't want to go out on a limb without more data."

Origin: Like climbing to a thin branch of a tree — you're far from safety and it could break.
```

---

## 📱 Message 3 — Your Turn

```
Your turn! ✍️

1) Write a sentence where someone goes out on a limb.

Example: "The investor went out on a limb and funded the startup when no one else would."

2) Bonus: Have you ever gone out on a limb for someone or something? What happened? 🎙️
```

---

## Teacher Notes
- Strong contrast pair with "play it safe" (not an idiom, but a natural phrase to teach alongside).
- Good for IELTS Task 2 essays on risk-taking or entrepreneurship.
