The **IELTS Reading Test** is a race against the clock. Unlike the Listening and Speaking tests, the **Reading module is different** depending on whether you are taking the **Academic** or **General Training** version.

Understanding these differences is critical for your preparation.

## The Golden Rule: Academic vs. General

- **Time:** Both tests are **60 minutes**.
- **Questions:** Both have **40 questions**.
- **Sections:** Both have **3 sections**.
- **The Difference:** The **TEXTS** you read and how you are **SCORED**.

> **Crucial Warning:** Unlike the Listening test, you do **NOT** get extra time to transfer your answers. You must write your answers directly onto the answer sheet within the 60 minutes.

---

## IELTS Academic Reading: For Students

**Purpose:** To test if you can handle university-level reading loads.

### The Structure

- **3 Long Passages:** Roughly 700–900 words each.
- **Total Words:** ~2,150–2,750 words total.
- **Source Material:** Books, journals, magazines, and newspapers.
- **Style:** Academic in style but written for a non-specialist audience. Topics range from biology and history to sociology and technology.
- **Difficulty:** High. You need a strong vocabulary and the ability to follow complex arguments.

### Strategic Tip

You don't need to be an expert in the topic to answer the questions. All the answers are in the text.

---

## IELTS General Training Reading: For Life & Work

**Purpose:** To test if you can survive in an English-speaking environment.

### The Structure

The General Training Reading test is divided into three distinct sections that get progressively harder.

- **Section 1 — "Social Survival":** Short, factual texts (advertisements, hotel notices, timetables, community guidelines). Focus: finding specific information quickly.
- **Section 2 — "Workplace Survival":** Two texts related to work (job descriptions, contracts, training manuals). Focus: understanding professional language.
- **Section 3 — "General Interest":** One long, complex text (newspaper or magazine article). Focus: reading for detail and following an argument.

---

## What About IELTS for UKVI?

**Academic UKVI Reading** = Standard Academic Reading. **General Training UKVI Reading** = Standard General Training Reading. The only difference is the security protocols at the test centre. Questions and texts are identical.

---

## The "Scoring Trap" (Important!)

Because the General Training texts are generally less complex, the grading scale is **tougher**. You need more correct answers to achieve the same Band Score.

**Approximate correct answers for Band 7.0:**

| Version | Correct Answers Needed |
|---|---|
| Academic Reading | ~30 / 40 |
| General Training Reading | ~34 / 40 |

> **Takeaway:** Do not underestimate General Training. You have less room for error!

## Common Question Types

1. **Multiple Choice:** Standard A, B, C, or D.
2. **True / False / Not Given:** Do the facts in the text agree with the statement?
   - *True:* Yes, the text says this.
   - *False:* The text says the opposite.
   - *Not Given:* The text does not mention this information (the "Ghost" trap).
3. **Yes / No / Not Given:** Same logic, but focuses on the **writer's opinion** rather than facts.
4. **Matching Headings:** Match a heading to the correct paragraph.
5. **Sentence Completion:** Fill in the blank with words from the text.
6. **Summary Completion:** Fill gaps in a summary of the text.
7. **Diagram Labelling:** Label parts of a machine or process described in the text.

## Academic vs. General — Summary

| Feature | Academic Reading | General Training Reading |
|---|---|---|
| Texts | 3 long academic passages | Section 1 (Social), Section 2 (Work), Section 3 (Long text) |
| Sources | Journals, textbooks, magazines | Notices, handbooks, newspapers |
| Vocabulary level | Advanced / abstract | Intermediate to advanced |
| Score needed for Band 7.0 | 30 / 40 | 34 / 40 |
| Main challenge | Vocabulary & complex ideas | Accuracy (high score required) |
