The **IELTS Writing Test** is often considered the most challenging part of the exam. Understanding the specific format is the key to achieving a Band 7.0 or higher.

## Quick Overview: The Basics

Regardless of which module you take, these rules always apply:

- **Total Time:** 60 minutes
- **Number of Tasks:** 2 (Task 1 and Task 2)
- **Marking:** Task 2 is worth **double** the marks of Task 1
- **Assessment:** Marked by certified examiners, not computers

> **Pro Tip:** Since Task 2 is worth 66% of your score, manage your time wisely. Spend **20 minutes on Task 1** and **40 minutes on Task 2**.

---

## IELTS Academic Writing Format

**Purpose:** University entry and professional registration (Doctors, Nurses, Engineers).

**Style:** Formal, objective, and academic.

### Task 1: Data Interpretation (Report Writing)

- **Format:** You are given a visual — a graph, chart, table, diagram, or map.
- **Goal:** Summarise the main features, select key data, and make comparisons where relevant.
- **Word Count:** Minimum **150 words**.
- **Key Skills:** Identifying trends, comparing data, describing processes. **Do not give your opinion.**

### Task 2: Academic Essay

- **Format:** A statement, point of view, or problem to discuss.
- **Common question types:**
  - Opinion (Do you agree or disagree?)
  - Discussion (Discuss both views and give your opinion)
  - Problem & Solution
  - Advantages & Disadvantages
- **Word Count:** Minimum **250 words**.
- **Key Skills:** Critical thinking, paragraph structure, supporting arguments with examples.

## IELTS General Training Writing Format

**Purpose:** Migration (Canada, Australia, UK) or work experience.

**Style:** Ranges from informal to semi-formal and formal.

### Task 1: Letter Writing

- **Format:** A common everyday situation requiring a letter to a specific person.
- **Goal:** Request information, explain a situation, or complain/apologise.
- **Letter types:**
  - **Formal:** To someone you don't know (e.g., a bank manager, a landlord).
  - **Semi-Formal:** To someone you know professionally (e.g., your manager).
  - **Informal:** To a friend or family member.
- **Word Count:** Minimum **150 words**.

### Task 2: General Essay

- **Format:** Similar to the Academic essay but topics are generally more social (e.g., family, TV, shopping, lifestyle).
- **Goal:** Provide your opinion following a standard essay structure. Slightly more personal tone is acceptable.
- **Word Count:** Minimum **250 words**.

## What About IELTS for UKVI?

The **IELTS for UKVI Writing test** is **identical** in questions, difficulty, format, scoring, and examiners. The only difference is the **security on test day** (video cameras, specific test centres) and the **Test Report Form (TRF)** number required by the UK Home Office. Prepare for it exactly the same way.

## Academic vs. General — Summary

| Feature | IELTS Academic | IELTS General Training |
|---|---|---|
| Task 1 | Describe a chart/graph (Report) | Write a letter (Formal/Informal) |
| Task 2 | Formal essay (Education, Environment, Tech) | General essay (Family, Lifestyle, Jobs) |
| Score weighting | Task 1 (33%) + Task 2 (66%) | Task 1 (33%) + Task 2 (66%) |
| Difficulty | Higher (academic vocabulary required) | Moderate (everyday English) |

## How Is Your Writing Scored?

Your writing is assessed on **4 criteria**, each worth **25%** of your band score:

1. **Task Achievement / Task Response:** Did you answer all parts of the question? Did you write enough words?
2. **Coherence & Cohesion:** Is your writing easy to follow? Do you use paragraphs and linking words (however, furthermore, consequently)?
3. **Lexical Resource (Vocabulary):** Do you use a wide range of words accurately? Do you use collocations?
4. **Grammatical Range & Accuracy:** Do you use a mix of simple and complex sentences? Is your grammar correct?
