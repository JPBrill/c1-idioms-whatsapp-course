## Exercise 1: True / False / Not Given

Read each statement. Based on what you learned in Lesson 1, write **True**, **False**, or **Not Given**.

1. The IELTS Listening test is different for Academic and General Training candidates.
2. You hear each audio recording twice.
3. The paper-based test gives you 10 extra minutes at the end to transfer your answers.
4. Section 4 is the easiest section in the Listening test.
5. You lose marks for wrong answers in the Listening test.
6. Computer-delivered candidates get 2 minutes to review their answers.
7. UKVI candidates take a different listening test than Academic candidates.
8. There are 10 questions per section.

---

## Exercise 2: Fill in the Blanks

Complete each sentence with **one or two words** from Lesson 1.

1. The IELTS Listening test has ________ sections.
2. Each section contains ________ questions.
3. Every correct answer is worth ________ mark.
4. Section 1 involves two speakers in a ________ context.
5. Section 4 is a university ________ with no breaks in the audio.
6. A raw score of approximately 30 correct answers gives a Band ________.
7. If a question says "No More Than Two Words," writing three words makes your answer ________.

## Exercise 3: Match the Section

Match each description (1–4) to the correct section (A–D).

**Descriptions:**
1. A student and tutor discussing a research project.
2. A guide explaining the layout of a science museum.
3. Two people making a hotel reservation by phone.
4. A professor delivering a lecture on climate change.

**Sections:**
- A. Section 1
- B. Section 2
- C. Section 3
- D. Section 4

## Exercise 4: Error Correction

Each sentence contains **one mistake** related to the Listening test. Find and correct it.

1. "In the paper-based test, you have 5 extra minutes to transfer your answers."
2. "Section 3 is a social monologue about everyday topics."
3. "If you spell a word incorrectly, you still receive the mark."
4. "The Listening test has 50 questions in total."
5. "In the computer-delivered test, you have 10 minutes to review your answers."

---

## Exercise 5: Short Answer — Exam Strategy

Answer each question in **no more than three words**.

1. What should you do if you don't know the answer to a Listening question?
2. What is the name of the technique where a speaker says one thing and then corrects themselves?
3. How many marks is each question in the Listening test worth?

## ✅ Answer Key

### Exercise 1
1. **False** — It is the same for all candidates.
2. **False** — You hear each recording once only.
3. **True**
4. **False** — Section 4 is the most difficult.
5. **False** — There is no negative marking.
6. **True**
7. **False** — UKVI uses the same listening test; only security protocols differ.
8. **True**

### Exercise 2
1. four
2. ten
3. one
4. social / everyday
5. lecture
6. 7.0
7. wrong / incorrect

### Exercise 3
1. C
2. B
3. A
4. D

### Exercise 4
1. **5** → **10** minutes
2. **Section 3** → **Section 2** (Section 2 is the social monologue)
3. "still receive" → **lose** the mark
4. **50** → **40** questions
5. **10** → **2** minutes

### Exercise 5
1. Always guess / never leave blank
2. The distractor
3. One mark
